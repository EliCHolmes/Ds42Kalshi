VISUALIZATION 1: Daily Political Trade Volume Over Time (Altair)
========================================================================

Design idea:
We chose an area chart (filled line chart) to show total daily trade volume
because it naturally emphasizes magnitude over time—the filled area draws the
eye to peaks and valleys more effectively than a bare line. The monotone
interpolation smooths visual noise from day-to-day fluctuations while
preserving the true data points.

Key design choices:
- Vertical dashed annotation lines mark key political events (Inauguration,
  funding deadlines). These provide context that transforms the chart from
  "here is volume" into "here is why volume changed."
- A two-panel brush+detail layout lets viewers explore macro trends in the
  top panel and zoom into specific date ranges in the bottom panel without
  losing overall context.
- Single-hue blue avoids unnecessary color complexity for a single series.

Alternatives considered:
- Bar chart (one bar per day): Rejected because bars create visual
  discontinuities that obscure the trend. The area fill communicates flow.
- Multi-line by topic: Rejected for this overview chart—too many overlapping
  lines. Topic breakdown is handled in Viz 6 instead.

Professor feedback incorporated:
- "Add in annotations—what happens on that day?" → Added vertical rules with
  event labels for Inauguration Day, government funding deadlines.
- "Introduce interactive plot" → Implemented brush selection for date-range
  zoom.

VISUALIZATION 2: Top 12 Markets by Volume, Yes/No Stacked (Altair)
========================================================================

Design idea:
A horizontal stacked bar chart was chosen because it handles long market
titles better than vertical bars (titles read naturally on the y-axis) and
the stacking directly shows how volume splits between Yes and No takers.

Key design choices:
- Blue (#4477AA) for Yes, coral (#EE6677) for No—distinguishable for all
  common forms of color vision deficiency (tested with Coblis CVD simulator).
- Markets sorted by total volume (descending from top) so the most important
  markets are seen first.
- Human-readable titles instead of raw ticker codes.
- Legend-click interaction to isolate Yes or No: this reveals which markets
  have balanced two-sided trading vs one-sided consensus.

Alternatives considered:
- Grouped (side-by-side) bars: Rejected because grouped bars make it harder
  to compare totals across markets. Stacking preserves the total comparison.
- Pie chart for Yes/No split: Rejected—pie charts are poor for precise
  comparison and violate DS4200 best practices.

Professor feedback incorporated:
- "Top 12 Markets—combine yes and no (stack)" → Directly implemented.
- "Look into type of events" → Market titles reveal event types.

VISUALIZATION 3: Trade Size Distribution by Market Topic (Altair)
========================================================================

Design idea:
Faceted histograms allow direct comparison of trade size distributions across
market topics without overplotting. Each facet uses independent y-axes since
topics have very different trade counts, ensuring that smaller categories
remain readable.

Key design choices:
- Colorblind-safe Tol palette (#4477AA, #EE6677, #228833, #CCBB44): avoids
  the red/green problem. No adjacent colors are confusable under common CVD.
- 30 bins balances detail with readability for the $2,000–$40,000+ range.
- Four main topics shown (Government Shutdown, Elections, International,
  Executive Orders); small categories omitted to reduce clutter.

Alternatives considered:
- Overlaid density plots: Rejected because overlapping densities are hard to
  read with 4+ categories and obscure the raw frequency counts.
- Violin plots: Considered, but histograms are more familiar to the audience
  and directly show counts.

Professor feedback incorporated:
- "Not red or green for colorblind" → Used the Tol colorblind-safe palette.
- "Change to shape" → Applied in Viz 5 (D3 scatter) where shape encoding
  is more effective; histograms use color per topic instead.

VISUALIZATION 4: Implied Probability vs Trade Value (Altair)
========================================================================

Design idea:
A scatter plot on a log-scaled y-axis reveals the relationship between how
certain the market is (implied probability) and how much capital is at stake.
The log scale is necessary because trade values span from $2,000 to $40,000+.

Key design choices:
- Sequential single-hue considerations: we use the Tol categorical palette
  for topic encoding rather than a rainbow gradient, per professor feedback.
  Color encodes topic, not a continuous variable.
- Point size encodes contract count, adding a third data dimension without
  requiring a third spatial axis.
- Brush selection lets viewers isolate regions of interest and see which
  topics dominate at different probability levels.

Alternatives considered:
- Heatmap/2D histogram: Rejected because individual trade identity matters—
  the tooltip reveals specific market titles.
- Linear y-axis: Rejected because the heavy right skew compresses most points
  to the bottom.

Professor feedback incorporated:
- "Volume by Implied—do not use rainbow, take color off" → No rainbow.
  Used categorical topic colors from the Tol palette.

VISUALIZATION 5: Interactive Trade Timeline Explorer (D3)
========================================================================

Design idea:
A scatter plot of individual trades over time, using shape encoding for
market topic, provides the most granular view of when and how big-money
trades occur. D3 enables smooth brush+zoom transitions.

Key design choices:
- Shape encoding for topic (circle, triangle, square, diamond, star, cross,
  wye): allows topic identification without relying solely on color—critical
  for accessibility.
- Log-scaled y-axis for trade value (same rationale as Viz 4).
- Dashed trend line (linear regression in log space) shows whether average
  trade size is trending up or down over the window.
- Brush to zoom + double-click to reset: the primary interaction pattern.
- Semi-transparent points (opacity 0.55) reduce overplotting for 7,646 points.

Alternatives considered:
- Jittered strip plot: Rejected—loses the temporal precision.
- Animated replay: Considered but adds complexity without analytical value.

Professor feedback incorporated:
- "Scatterplot—change to shape" → Shape encodes market topic.
- "Not red or green for colorblind" → Same Tol palette as all other charts.
- "Indication line to highlight trend" → Dashed regression line overlaid.

VISUALIZATION 6: Market Deep Dive – Price & Volume Over Time (D3)
========================================================================

Design idea:
A dual-axis chart combining daily volume (bars, left axis) and average
implied probability (line, right axis) reveals how conviction and capital
co-evolve for a specific political topic. The dropdown interaction lets
viewers compare structural patterns across topics.

Key design choices:
- Blue bars for volume, red line for probability: distinct visual channels
  (bar vs line) plus distinct colors ensure they are never confused.
- Dropdown selector (not radio buttons or tabs): scales well to 7 topics
  and is a familiar interaction pattern.
- Animated transitions on topic change help viewers track what changed.

Alternatives considered:
- Separate charts for volume and probability: Rejected—the dual-axis format
  directly reveals correlation/decorrelation between the two metrics.
- Small multiples (one per topic): Considered, but the interactive dropdown
  uses less space and focuses attention.

Professor feedback incorporated:
- "Use one topic with related questions" → Dropdown scopes to one topic.
- "Maybe introduce interactive plot → allows viewer to choose the topic"
  → Dropdown selector.
- "What policies/events occurred on that day?" → Volume spikes in context
  with probability shifts reveal event impacts.

VISUALIZATION 7: Trade Activity Heatmap – Day of Week × Hour (D3)
========================================================================

Design idea:
A heatmap naturally encodes a 2D categorical grid (day × hour) with color
intensity. This reveals temporal trading patterns invisible in other charts.

Key design choices:
- Sequential single-hue blues: the simplest effective color scale for a
  single quantitative variable. No rainbow, no diverging scale (there is
  no meaningful midpoint).
- Rounded corners on cells for a cleaner aesthetic.
- Abbreviated day names (Mon, Tue...) and AM/PM hour labels for readability.
- Gradient legend with axis for precise value lookup.

Alternatives considered:
- Calendar heatmap (GitHub-style): Rejected because it shows specific dates,
  not the day-of-week × hour pattern we want to reveal.
- 3D surface plot: Rejected—gratuitous 3D adds no information and violates
  DS4200 design principles.

Professor feedback incorporated:
- General principle: "No unnecessary 3D" → Used 2D heatmap.
- "Do not use rainbow" → Sequential blues only.

VISUALIZATION 8: Market Category Breakdown (D3)
========================================================================

Design idea:
A bar chart comparing market topic categories across switchable metrics
(total volume, trade count, average trade size) prevents the common mistake
of drawing conclusions from a single metric. The dropdown interaction reveals
that volume leaders are not necessarily the categories with the largest
individual trades.

Key design choices:
- Consistent Tol topic colors matching all other visualizations.
- Animated bar transitions when switching metrics help viewers track
  how categories re-rank.
- Value labels on bars for precise reading without requiring axis lookup.
- Tooltip includes unique market count—an important context variable not
  shown in the bars.

Alternatives considered:
- Treemap: Considered for showing hierarchical structure, but the flat
  category list does not have a natural hierarchy, and treemaps are harder
  to read precisely.
- Stacked bar (all metrics): Rejected because the three metrics have
  different units and scales; stacking them would be misleading.

Professor feedback incorporated:
- "Look into countries, type of events" → Market topics include
  "International Affairs" which groups country-focused markets; the tooltip
  shows unique market counts per topic.
- "Thinking about color blindness" → Same Tol palette throughout.
